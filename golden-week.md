# ⚠️ Golden Week — Read This First

Golden Week is Japan's biggest domestic travel event of the year. Your trip ends May 1, so you only catch the first 3 days of it — but April 29 (USJ day) falls right on Showa Day, the first Golden Week holiday. Plan accordingly.

---

## What Is Golden Week?

A cluster of four national holidays in quick succession:

| Date | Holiday |
|------|---------|
| April 29 | Showa Day |
| May 3 | Constitution Day |
| May 4 | Greenery Day |
| May 5 | Children's Day |

In 2026 this runs **April 29 – May 5**, with the travel surge starting **April 26–27** as people begin departing. It is the single busiest travel period in Japan — tens of millions of Japanese take domestic vacations simultaneously.

---

## The Hard Realities

- **Accommodation prices 2–3x** in Kyoto, Hakone, Nikko, Nara, and Osaka
- **Shinkansen reserved seats sell out** weeks in advance — especially the Tokaido line (Tokyo–Osaka)
- **Major attractions hit capacity** — Fushimi Inari, Kiyomizu-dera, Universal Studios Japan can have 2+ hour queues or timed entry limits
- **Restaurants** at popular spots can have 1–2 hour waits without reservations
- **Everything is more expensive and more crowded** — but Japan is still extremely manageable if you plan around it

---

## What To Do About It

### Book Accommodation Now

This is not an exaggeration. Golden Week accommodation in Kyoto, Hakone, and Osaka sells out **months in advance**. If you're reading this close to your trip dates, check immediately and book whatever is available.

### Lock In Shinkansen Reservations

JR opens reservations exactly **1 month in advance**. Set reminders:

- March 29 → book any April 29 travel
- March 30 → book April 30 travel
- etc.

Book via the **SmartEX app** (English available) or at JR ticket offices.

### Use Golden Week Strategically

**Best cities during Golden Week:**

| City | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| **Tokyo** | Japanese workers *leave* Tokyo — business hotels can actually be cheaper. Tourist spots are crowded but manageable. |
| **Hiroshima** | Typically manageable even during Golden Week |
| **Kanazawa** | Much less tourist traffic than Kyoto despite being beautiful |

**Worst cities during Golden Week:**

| City | Problem |
|------|---------|
| **Kyoto** | The single most overcrowded destination. Human traffic jams at popular spots. |
| **Hakone** | Small resort town completely overrun; Mt. Fuji view spots packed |
| **Nikko** | Popular domestic destination, very crowded |
| **Nara** | Small, doesn't absorb crowds well |

### Your Structure Around Golden Week

```
April 22–26 → Tokyo (arrive, explore, day trips — before Golden Week)
April 27     → Shinkansen to Kyoto (ahead of the surge)
April 28     → Kyoto → Nara → Osaka (arrive before GW peak)
April 29     → USJ (Showa Day — buy Express Pass, arrive at opening)
April 30     → Osaka free day (GW is active but Osaka handles it better than Kyoto)
May 1        → Depart KIX (GW still running, but you're out)
```

The good news: you leave before the worst of it (May 3–5). Your biggest Golden Week exposure is USJ on April 29 — which is why the Express Pass is non-negotiable.

### Visit Major Sights Before 8 AM

At popular Kyoto sites, the difference between 6:30 AM and 10:00 AM is the difference between an incredible experience and a miserable queue. Fushimi Inari at dawn is magical. At midday it's shoulder-to-shoulder.

---

## The Silver Lining

Golden Week also means **festivals**. Many matsuri (street festivals, shrine events, traditional performances) happen throughout the week. It's genuinely lively and culturally interesting — just on a different level than a quieter time of year.

Japan handles crowds better than almost any other country. It will still be amazing. Just plan around it.
