# Budget — How Much Does Japan Actually Cost?

> Exchange rate reference: ~¥150 = $1 USD (verify closer to your trip)

---

## The Short Answer

| Tier | Total Per Person (2 weeks incl. flights) | Daily In-Country |
|------|------------------------------------------|------------------|
| Budget | $2,500 – $4,000 | $65–110/day |
| Mid-Range | $5,000 – $8,000 | $150–250/day |
| Comfortable | $8,000 – $14,000 | $300–500/day |

**Most realistic for a first trip doing it right without going broke: $4,000–6,000/person.**

---

## Flights (Round-Trip, Economy, From US)

| Scenario | Cost Per Person |
|----------|----------------|
| Book early, West Coast departure | $600–900 |
| Average/typical | $900–1,200 |
| Last-minute or East Coast | $1,200–1,600+ |

**Tips:**
- West Coast cities (LAX, SFO, SEA) are significantly cheaper to fly from
- East Coast adds roughly $200–400
- Set fare alerts on Google Flights, Kayak, and Hopper months out
- Flying into Tokyo (Narita/Haneda) and out of Osaka (Kansai) — called an "open-jaw" ticket — eliminates the need to backtrack and can save money overall
- Late April is shoulder season for flights — not peak, not cheapest
- **2026 note:** Japan is raising its tourist arrival tax from ¥1,000 to ¥3,000 (~$20/person)

## JR Pass — Is It Worth It?

The 14-day nationwide JR Pass costs **¥80,000 (~$533/person)**. Run the math on your specific route before buying.

**Rough Shinkansen costs for a typical route (Tokyo → Kyoto → Hiroshima → Osaka → Tokyo):**

| Leg | Cost Without Pass |
|-----|------------------|
| Tokyo → Kyoto | ¥13,320 |
| Kyoto → Hiroshima | ¥11,420 |
| Hiroshima → Osaka | ¥10,420 |
| Osaka → Tokyo | ¥13,870 |
| **Subtotal** | **¥49,030 (~$327)** |

At ¥49,030 in Shinkansen alone, the 14-day pass at ¥80,000 doesn't pay off unless you add significant regional rail travel.

**Often better value:**
- Buy individual Shinkansen tickets via the **SmartEX app** (slight discount, flexible booking)
- Add a **JR Kansai Wide Area Pass** (¥12,000 / 5 days) for Kyoto/Osaka/Hiroshima/Nara/Himeji region
- Use IC card for all city transit
- **Total: ~¥25,000–35,000 vs ¥80,000 for the pass**

Use the [Japan Guide Rail Pass Calculator](https://www.japan-guide.com/railpass/) with your exact itinerary.

## Budget Strategy: Getting the Most Value

**Eat like this:**
- Breakfast → convenience store (¥300–500)
- Lunch → big meal at a sit-down restaurant (lunch sets are 30–50% cheaper than dinner)
- Dinner → ramen, izakaya, or supermarket bento with evening discount sticker

**Sleep like this:**
- Most nights → business hotel or private guesthouse room (split between two people = $35–65 each)
- 1–2 nights → ryokan (splurge, worth it, budget ¥20,000–40,000/person for one night with meals)
- Optional → capsule hotel for 1 night as an experience

**Travel like this:**
- Skip the 14-day JR Pass unless your itinerary proves it pays off
- Buy individual Shinkansen tickets in advance via SmartEX
- IC card for all local transit
- Consider an overnight highway bus from Tokyo→Osaka once ($30–40) to save both transport cost and a night's accommodation
